The commerce use case

When agents transact at scale,
someone has to verify the human behind them.

By 2027, more digital transactions will be initiated by agents than by humans. Every one of those transactions carries a question the payment stack was never designed to answer: did the human actually authorize this?

Without cryptographic proof, the answer is a log file and a prayer. Chargeback exposure becomes unbounded. Disputes become probabilistic. Retailers eat the loss.

Sovereign delegation credentials change the shape of the problem. The human signs once — scope, spend limit, duration, approved counterparties. The agent presents the credential at transaction time. The platform verifies it on-chain. When a dispute arrives, the platform has deterministic proof of authorization.
