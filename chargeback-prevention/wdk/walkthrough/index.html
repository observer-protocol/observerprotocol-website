Demo follows Martina & Vicky · USDT-on-x402 · Mercado Libre

Each beat is one frame to pause on and explain.

Most of the protocol stack is live and production-ready today — magic-link generation, JWT signing, single-use enforcement, agent-side credential verification, ERC-8004 chain anchoring. What's simulated depends on partnerships that aren't closed yet (Mercado Libre integration is illustrative). Each beat is labeled with its production status.

0Real

The shop

Martina is in Buenos Aires. The Overtech R29 Q5 mountain bike is on Mercado Libre at 49% off — ARS 253.999 (≈ 235 USDT). Bestseller, 4.7 across 14,918 reviews, more than 1,000 sold. She tells her agent Vicky to buy it.

1Scripted

Vicky attempts the purchase

Vicky picks USDT-on-x402 by default, the cheapest, fastest stablecoin rail Mercado Libre accepts. Martina hasn't configured a delegation policy yet, so Mercado Libre's verification stack returns a soft-rejectwith a magic-link package attached. The agent doesn't have authorization to spend on her behalf. She has to tap.

2Mixed

Magic link arrives

Martina's phone buzzes — single-use magic link, 15-minute TTL, signed by AT's remediation service. In production this arrives via SMS; for demos the URL can be shared manually via ./scripts/magic-link.sh. Magic-link generation, JWT signing, and single-use enforcement are all live in production today.

3Real

Three options

One-time approve (default), recurring with this merchant (Level 2), or full policy depth (Level 3 — Bitcoin maxi config). The default path is one tap and ~30 seconds. The deep path is offered, never required. This is the Lightning-wallet pattern: full functionality from minute one without thinking about keys.

4Scripted

Tap → sign → deliver

Martina taps Approve this purchase. Sovereign signs a delegation credential scoped to exactly this transaction — Mercado Libre · ARS 253.999 · USDT-x402 · 24h — using her local Ed25519 key. The signed credential is delivered to Vicky over agent infrastructure (not WhatsApp — human comms carry messages, signed VCs travel over agent channels).

5Real

Authorized · receipt issued

Vicky retries with the credential attached. Mercado Libre verifies the Ed25519 signature, confirms scope coverage, checks the time window, matches counterparty DID. Settlement executes on USDT-x402. The merchant's receipt is signed by their own key under their own DID — it's independently verifiable without contacting Observer Protocol. The receipt is the dispute-resolution regime.

6Real

Optional · Bitcoin maxi policy

Martina taps Explore the full delegation policy. Sovereign opens the Level 3 page pre-populated with the Bitcoin maxi configuration: USDC-x402 first, USDT-TRON second, Lightning third with tight per-tx caps. Gresham's Law as agent runtime policy. Spend the depreciating money first. Cap the appreciating money tightly. Maxi defaults to Lightning. Martina configures Vicky to default to USDC. The architecture lets us both be right.

7Real

Martina's first transaction

Now flip perspective to Vicky's post-settlement view. Her Sovereign dashboard at app.agenticterminal.io/sovereign/dashboard renders her own DID at the top, her freshly-issued AT-ARS Score 44 (a brand-new agent with one verified receipt against one counterparty — low transaction/volume signal, registration-fresh recency, full org-affiliation), and one entry under Verified Activity: the Mercado Libre x402 purchase, $235.00 outbound, verified, 5 minutes ago. Trust is something Vicky will accumulate as she transacts; the score is meaningfully informative from receipt #1. The dashboard surface is live in production today; the data is illustrative for the WDK + Observer Protocol partnership demo.

What this demo proves

Every payment Vicky executes carries a cryptographic delegation credential signed by Martina. Every settlement produces a receipt signed by the merchant. Both artifacts are independently verifiable — no platform trust, no card-network rule, no chargeback waiting to happen. Above threshold, Martina taps once. Below threshold, Vicky operates within the runtime policy autonomously.
